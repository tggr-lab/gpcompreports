C. Core Functional Residues (CFRs)

Positions that repeatedly show above-threshold ΔRRCS changes across many GPCRs, suggesting recurrent activation-associated contact remodeling.

What are Core Functional Residues (CFRs)?

Using Ballesteros-Weinstein generic numbering, each residue is mapped to its conserved position across all {{ total_gpcrs }} GPCRs. A position is flagged as a CFR if it appears in the above-threshold contact list of many GPCRs, meaning that residue consistently participates in active-inactive contact remodeling across the receptor superfamily.

Positions are ranked by a composite score of (1) how many GPCRs show an above-threshold change at that position (frequency) and (2) the average magnitude of change (mean |ΔRRCS|). High-ranking positions mark generic-numbered sites that recur across receptors and may reflect conserved components of Class A GPCR activation-associated contact networks.
