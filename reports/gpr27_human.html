Methods

RRCS analysis. Residue-Residue Contact Scores (RRCS) quantify pairwise residue contact strength from inter-atomic distances; comparing active and inactive structures predicted by AlphaFold multistate (del Alamo et al., 2022) gives a per-pair ΔRRCS.

Receptor-specific ΔRRCS threshold. |ΔRRCS| ≥ 2.62, computed as max(mean(|Δ|) + σ, 0.2). The 0.2 floor ensures receptors with very small overall change still surface their largest contacts.

Variant data. Population frequencies from gnomAD v4; pathogenicity predictions from AlphaMissense; conservation from ProtVar / UniProt. The Het count column shows heterozygote allele counts; homozygote counts are uniformly zero across these rare missense variants in the current gnomAD v4 snapshot and are omitted.

Structural annotation. TM domain boundaries and generic numbering from GPCRdb.

What is RRCS?

Residue-Residue Contact Score (RRCS) measures how strongly two amino acids interact in a protein structure. When a protein changes shape (from inactive to active), these contact strengths change.

ΔRRCS (Delta RRCS) shows the difference in contact strength between states:

  • Positive ΔRRCS. Contact is stronger in the active state.
  • Negative ΔRRCS. Contact is stronger in the inactive state.
  • Large |ΔRRCS|. Notable contact-score difference between the active and inactive models.

Residues with large ΔRRCS values mark positions whose contact environment differs between the active and inactive models. These positions may be useful candidates for structural interpretation or follow-up variant analysis, but they should not be interpreted as functional proof on their own.

Pathogenicity predictions

AlphaMissense scores estimate the likelihood that a missense substitution disrupts function, based on structural and evolutionary features. The published score thresholds bin each variant as:

  • Pathogenic (score ≥ 0.564): consistent with disruptive substitutions in known disease genes.
  • Ambiguous (0.34–0.563): score does not clearly favor either class.
  • Benign (< 0.34): consistent with tolerated substitutions.

Conservation & population data

Conservation score. How well-preserved a position is across species (0 = variable, 1 = conserved). Highly conserved positions are often functionally important, but conservation alone is not proof of function.

Allele frequency. Population frequency of the variant in gnomAD v4. Rare variants are common across the genome and many are undersampled; rarity alone is not evidence of pathogenicity.
