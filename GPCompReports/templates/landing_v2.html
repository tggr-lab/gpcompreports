    How it works

    Four stages from structural models to variant-annotated contact changes.

    1. 01

      AlphaFold multistate

      Both active and inactive conformations predicted for each receptor.

    2. 02

      RRCS per pair

      Residue–residue contact score computed for every pair in both states.

    3. 03

      Δ analysis

      Signed differences surface contacts that gain or lose on activation.

    4. 04

      Variant overlay

      gnomAD allele frequencies and AlphaMissense pathogenicity mapped to Δ-hotspots.

    About

    GPCRsynth computes residue–residue contact scores across both conformational states of every human Class A GPCR, using AlphaFold multistate predicted structures (del Alamo et al., 2022). Contacts are mapped to Ballesteros–Weinstein numbering via GPCRdb.

    Signed ΔRRCS values are overlaid with population variant data from gnomAD v4 and pathogenicity predictions from AlphaMissense, enabling identification of Core Functional Residues (CFRs) conserved across the superfamily and exploration of structure–function–variant relationships.
